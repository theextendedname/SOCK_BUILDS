Holosmith DPS
<:engineer_holosmith:1162443831837462539>Holosmith DPS
- Credit: @Alyrico
- [The Build](<http://gw2skills.net/editor/?PegAQRlJw8YRsOGKO2LvvXA-DWRYih7bOqQgFSTFRmpQFRgtnFCQA7h3i+nRAA-w>)
- [Video](<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t6ina4taroY>)
- More info
 - Use Light Density Amplifier instead of Solar Focusing Lens if you need more survivability. Replace Flamethrower with Elixir S if you still need more survivability or if you find yourself dying off tag from cc.
 - Use the rifle with bloodlust sigil in fights until you get 25 stacks of bloodlust. Once you have your stacks, use the rifle with the force sigil. 
 - Your first goal in fights should be to have over 50 heat when the fight starts. Prime Light Beam (PLB) requires you to be above 50 heat to leave its damaging aoe, so you should generally try to be above 50 heat when this skill is not on cooldown so that you are always ready to use it. Photon Forge 4 and 5 are good options to quickly raise your heat, although make sure not to use Forge 5 if you need to stay on the move as it will lock you in place. Make sure to never overheat when playing this build.
 - You want to start fights in Photon Forge, and you should aim to land Forge 3 and PLB before leaving. You typically want to start fights by using Forge 2 as you get near the enemies for superspeed and then using Forge 3 in melee range. Fill time with Forge auto attacks until the enemy group slows down or stops moving and then use PLB on their stack. Starting fights with PLB first can be good if the enemy group has little room to move out of the aoe or if the enemy pushes you through a choke. If the enemy group doesn't give you an opening or it's too dangerous to lock yourself in place with PLB, move on to the next part of the rotation and use PLB whenever you see an opening as long as you have more than 50 heat left.
 - After using Forge 3 and PLB you want to use your Rifle and Elixir Gun. The important skills to use here are Rifle 2, Rifle 5, and Elixir Gun 4. Rifle is your best follow-up option if you need a gap closer or the enemy is on the move. Leap onto enemies with Rifle 5 and then immediately hit them again with Rifle 2. When enemies are stationary, Elixir Gun is your best follow-up option. Casting Elixir Gun 4 will cause your character to fly backwards, so hit your weapon swap key immediately after casting the skill to cancel the movement. Once you've used all three of these skills, return to Photon Forge and use Forge 3 + auto attacks. Do this until you are close to overheating and then cycle back through your Rifle and Elixir Gun abilities. Your goal during the fight will be to constantly swap between these three sets of weapon abilities as they become available.
 - There are two other damage abilities that need to be worked into your rotation: Laser Disk and Blade Burst. Blade Burst is an instant cast skill that hits nearby enemies, so try to combo it with a heavy-hitting skill like Forge 3 or Rifle 5. This ability is a projectile, so try not to use it if you see any projectile hate nearby. Laser Disk does constant damage in an area around you, so use this skill at a moment in a fight when you'll be staying right on top of the enemies. Try to use Laser Disk when you have over 50 heat so that it lasts for a longer duration.
 - The Flamethrower is used in this build to fill in any gaps in your damage output. Use the Flamethrower auto-attack when you have too much heat to use Photon Forge and all your other damage skills are on cooldown, or if you can hit enemies with the Flamethrower auto-attack but are not in range to use your other skills. Always prioritze your other damaging skills over Flamethrower.




Scourge Condi Boon Strip

- Credit: @Alyrico
- [The Build](<http://gw2skills.net/editor/?PSwAEt/lNweYSMEmJW0T9PVA-D2QYrw+RN+MyUJ0hMQhHECQ46hXDIOrAA-w>)
- [Video](<https://youtu.be/b1VOhApubOU?si=MyGNwGTylkmN-bBd>)
- More info
 - This is a condi build designed to put maximum condition pressure alongside Mystic Rebuke Firebrands. As necromancer, we still focus on boon strips in our gameplay. As a tertiary goal, we are taking Transfusion and other traits aimed to revive our downs faster.
 - We start the fights in our scepter/torch set, all the skills are useful, pay particular attention to Scepter 3 as a powerful boon corruption. As we are about to engage in melee, Trail of Anguish and Ghastly breach should be your priority. Use Well of Corruption on command. Corrosive poison Cloud negates projectiles and inflict conditions, it can be used at range like other wells or in melee. Use Sand Flare on cooldown when in melee. GS set, top priorities are, GS4 just before or in melee and Gravedigger when you see some downs, don't wait for the commander call, swap immediately to GS.
 - Shades. These are not your top priority, just use F2, F3 and F5 off cooldown. Always keep at least one shade up. 
 - Do NOT use F4 unless someone from your party is down. We will be running several scourges, so it's important we don't waste F4 for the same person.
