### Guild Wars 2: WvW Druid Class Guide

Druids are superb healers and cleansers, providing essential boons like Fury and Protection. They offer massive burst healing but have a **medium/high difficulty** curve due to ground targets, positioning, and combo requirements.

### Gear & Build Flexibility

* **Armor:** Exotic gear is perfectly fine for WvW (purchasable with WvW currency).
* **Runes:** **Superior Rune of the Water** (Cheaper alternative to Monk runes and can be crafted).
* **Relic:** Your relic heals allies (not you) on blast finishers. *Note: A minor trait will heal you whenever you heal allies.*


### Essential Target Settings

1. **Turn OFF Autotargeting** in your game settings.
2. Create keybinds for **"Select Personal Target"** and **"Call Personal Target"**. Mark an ally in your party with this.
3. Use **Staff 2** directly on that friendly target to bounce heals.


### Celestial Avatar (CA) Mechanics

CA is your elite mechanic (the light-blue sphere/bar above your health). It runs on **Astral Force (AF)**, generated by damaging enemies and healing allies.

* **Entering CA:** Can only enter when AF is full. Entering triggers an area condition cleanse and counts as a weapon swap.
* **The Timer:** You have 15 seconds in CA. You can spam skills (especially Skill 1, a ground-target heal) during this time.
* **Leaving CA:** Exiting puts CA on a 10s cooldown (reduced by traits/Alacrity). You can leave early, but you will lose half of your remaining AF.


### Weapon Skill Priorities

**Quick Draw Trait:** Your first weapon skill used after swapping weapons (or entering/exiting CA) gets a **66% cooldown reduction**.

1. **Celestial Avatar:** Enter a few seconds into combat. Stay in as long as possible.
* **Combo:** Skill 3 first (let it channel completely) -> Skills 4 + 2 -> Spam 1 & 4.
* **AVOID Skill 5:** It roots you in place. Using it while the group is moving will get you killed.


2. **Staff:** Swap to Staff and immediately use **Staff 3** at your feet (preferably into a water/light field). Use **Staff 4** on enemies, **Staff 5** ahead of group movement (blast it with Staff 3 if possible), and **Staff 2** on your personal ally target.
3. **Mace / Warhorn:** Use **Warhorn 5** before combat for pre-boons. In combat, use **Warhorn 5** (or **Mace 3** if safe) immediately after swapping, followed by **Mace 2**, **Mace 3**, and **Warhorn 4** for extra cleansing.


### Utility & Spirit Priorities

* **Utility Glyphs:** Only use these *inside* CA for heals/cleanses. **Exception:** *Glyph of Equality* is a group stunbreak; use it anytime it's needed. Stick close to your tag, as glyphs only affect nearby allies.
* **Heal Skill:** Use outside of CA to cleanse/heal. Blast its water field if you can.
* **Frost Spirit:** Strong double-cleanse and boon producer. Cast on engage in front of the group so they walk into it. Use on cooldown outside CA.
* **Nature Spirit:** Massive double-revive with a long cooldown. Use when teammates are downed or you desperately need condition clears. (Very slow 3-second cast time; easily interrupted).


### Optional Flex Slots (Replacing Glyph of Alignment)

* **Stone Spirit:** Provides Aegis, Protection, and a double blast finisher. (Highly recommended).
* **Signet of Renewal:** Bring this if facing extremely condition-heavy enemy comps.
* **Protect Me:** Less optimal, but an easy panic button requiring less thought.
* **Glyph of the Stars (Elite alternative to Nature Spirit):** Excellent flexibility. Outside CA, it provides Stability, cleanses, and a water field. Inside CA, it boosts healing and boons.


### Pets

* **Pet 1:** **Brown Bear** (Always best for WvW).
* **Pet 2 (Flex):** * *River Otter:* Extra Fury, healing, and enemy Weakness debuffs.
* *Pink Moa:* Good budget choice if you lack the Otter.
* *Smokescale:* Great for dropping a smoke field.
* *Fernhound:* Decent synergy with minor healing and Regen.
* *White Moa:* Provides Frost Aura for damage reduction.


### Critical Positioning & Micro-Tips

* **Staff 3 Warning:** If there is skill lag while you are exiting CA, spamming Staff 3 can accidentally "yeet" you wildly out of position. Be conscious of your AF bar.
* **Easy Blast Finishers:** Warhorn 5, Mace 3, Staff 3, CA 3.
* **Mace 3 Trick:** *Oaken Cudgel (Mace 3)* is a blast finisher but leaps you forward. You can **animation cancel the movement by pressing weapon swap**, dropping the blast perfectly at your feet.
* Weapon swapping cleanses only you, not your allies. Stick tight to your tag!


##  "Beginner" Heal Druid Rotation

**Pre-Fight**

Warhorn 5 (on might call)  >  WEAPON SWAP >  Frost Spirit (placed just ahead of group timing it to cast as we engage)

**Core Rotation**

Staff 3 (on feet) > Staff 4 (towards enemy) > Staff 5 (ahead) + Staff 3 (blast) > Staff 2 (on Personal Target) 

WEAPON SWAP

Warhorn > Mace 3 [SITUATIONAL] > Mace 2 > Mace 3 + Warhorn 4 > Celestial Avatar

[Don't cast Mace 3 if the leap is BAD]

**Celestial Avatar Rotation **

CA 3  > CA 4  > CA 2  > CA 1 through CA 4 off cool down until astral force is consumed. 

(Cast CA 3 again as soon as it’s available)

**Notes**
- Other utilities and pet skills are slotted in as needed/off cooldown. See guidance above. 
- *Healing increases with actions per min (APM) and prioritizing CA.*
